# Phase 1: Analysis Framework (excerpt, Deer-Flow `consulting-analysis` skill)
Source: https://github.com/bytedance/deer-flow/blob/main/skills/public/consulting-analysis/SKILL.md

## Phase 1 Purpose
Given a **research subject**, produce a complete **analysis framework** as the blueprint for data collection and final report generation.

## Data Authenticity (Phase 2 reminder)
- All data must come from **Data Summary** or **External Search**; if missing, state "Data not available" — do not fabricate.
- Traces: major claims and charts must be traceable to inputs.

## Step 1.1: Understand the Research Subject
- Core entity (market, brand, product, industry segment, etc.)
- Analytical domain (marketing, finance, industry, consumer, investment, etc.)
- Natural dimensions (market size, growth, segmentation, competition, etc.)

## Step 1.2: Select Analysis Frameworks
Choose **2-4** complementary, domain-appropriate frameworks. Examples: SWOT, PEST/PESTEL, Porter Five Forces, STP, TAM-SAM-SOM, BCG, Consumer Decision Journey, DCF, value chain, benchmarking, etc. Prefer **depth over breadth**.

Output a **Framework Selection** table: Chapter | Framework(s) | Application.

## Step 1.3: Design Chapter Skeleton
Each chapter must include:
1. **Chapter Title** — professional, concise
2. **Analysis Objective** — what this chapter aims to reveal
3. **Analysis Logic** — reasoning chain; **must name** the frameworks from Step 1.2
4. **Core Hypothesis** — hypotheses to validate with data
5. **Data Requirements** — what to collect
6. **Visualization Plan** — chart/table ideas (no fake numbers in framework phase)

## Output style
Consulting voice (McKinsey/BCG-like clarity). For Chinese reports use professional `zh_CN` when user locale is Chinese.
